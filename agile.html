Agile PM

Agile project management is known for delivering work in quick bursts, also known as sprints. These sprints typically last only a few weeks, commonly only two. These sprints are repeated until the deliverable has met the clients requirements. Agile also does not usually start with any pre-defined requirements. Essentially, the team takes an idea and begin the process.

The goal here is to be able to deliver something based off a rough idea to then clarify, define and refine requirements for the next sprint. This creates and encourages collaboration between the team and the client, encouraging frequent communication and brainstorming, while defining the requirements along the way. This opens the field for changes and innovative ideas during development. It avoids delivering a client a final product, who in return says they want to go in a different direction. This saves the development team and the client time and money.
